🔍Pool growth between snapshots
For each pair of consecutive pool snapshots: take the earlier total, subtract the invitations issued by draws in between (candidates removed from the pool), then compare that expected number to the actual later total. The difference is how many new candidates joined the pool in that window.
📊New candidates by CRS band
Same idea, split by band (601-1200, 501-600, 451-500, 401-450, 351-400, 301-350, 0-300). Model: each draw's invitations are skimmed from the top band downward until its cutoff is reached (how ITAs are actually issued), then leftover vs. the actual next snapshot = new candidates in that band. Category-based / PNP rounds with unusual cutoffs can skew this — treat as an estimate, not exact.
🌱Pool growth per band
One column per pool snapshot date. Each cell is the raw change in that band vs. the previous snapshot — no draws math, just how the published count moved. Updates automatically whenever a new date is added on Pool breakdown.
